## 2. Semantic decisions I made (REQUIRED)

Explain at least **three semantic choices** you made in your HTML.

### Decision 1
- Element(s) used: `<header>` and `<h1>`
- Where in the page: At the top of the page
- Why this element is semantically correct: I used `<header>` for the introduction part of the page, and I used exactly one `<h1>` for my name as the main heading.

### Decision 2
- Element(s) used: `<section>` and `<article>`
- Where in the page: In the portfolio section
- Why this element is semantically correct: I used `<section>` to group major content areas and `<article>` to separate different parts of the portfolio, such as links, video, and project images.

### Decision 3
- Element(s) used: `<figure>` and `<figcaption>`
- Where in the page: Around the project images and embedded video
- Why this element is semantically correct: I used `<figure>` and `<figcaption>` because they are appropriate for media content and provide descriptions for the images and video.

*(Example: Why `<section>` instead of `<div>`, why `<article>` here, why this heading level, etc.)*

## 3. Accessibility considerations
- I added a skip link at the top of the page so keyboard users can jump directly to the main content.
- I added alt text for all images so screen reader users can understand the image content.
- I used a clear heading hierarchy with `<h1>`, `<h2>`, and `<h3>`.
- I used semantic HTML elements instead of unnecessary `<div>` elements.

How do they improve usability?
- These accessibility features make the page easier to navigate, easier to understand, and more usable for people using keyboards or screen readers.
